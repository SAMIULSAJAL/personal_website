Research

Thesis Title: "Formation and oxidation of nano transition metals in the laser ablation technique by the ReaxFF reactive force field using Molecular Dynamics in High-Performance Computers"

Abstract: In this work, we perform Molecular Dynamics simulations on high-performance computers (HPC) to investigate the phase transitions of bulk aluminum in an aqueous environment. The high-intensity laser bombardment on aluminum leads to plasma formation on the surface, which interacts with the laser beam. We aim to observe the mechanism of phase transformation on the picosecond time scale.

Link: Read Thesis (PDF)

DOI: 10.13140/RG.2.2.21838.43845

Key Techniques: Molecular Dynamics (MD), ReaxFF reactive force field, LAMMPS, VMD, OVITO visualization tools.

Ongoing Research: We are currently analyzing the thermodynamical properties that affect nano-cluster formation and oxidation mechanisms.

Final Reports

How is Internet and News Consumption Connected to Attitudes Towards Climate Change?

This report analyzes the correlation between internet usage, news consumption, and climate change attitudes across all countries in the ESS dataset. The study applies univariate and bivariate statistical analysis methods to explore the relationships between these variables.

Key Findings: The report includes graphical representations of the data and statistical tests for nominal, ordinal, and metric variables, showing significant relationships between internet usage and climate change opinions.

Results & Analysis:
- Univariate analysis: Mean, median, mode calculations for key variables.
- Bivariate analysis: Correlation between internet usage and news consumption towards climate change.
- Insights into the relationship between age and internet usage time.
